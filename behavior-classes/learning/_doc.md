# Learning

> The process of acquiring new knowledge and skills, the core driver of human progress.

## Rules

Guidelines to follow when executing learning behaviors:

1. **Clear Objectives**: Define clear learning objectives before each session
2. **Time Limits**: Learning sessions should not exceed 90 minutes, must take breaks
3. **Timely Summary**: Must write summary notes after completing learning
4. **Regular Review**: Follow the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve - review at 1 day / 3 days / 7 days / 30 days
5. **Output Oriented**: Must have output after learning (notes, practice, teaching others)

## Experiences

Accumulated experiences and insights about learning:

- The Pomodoro Technique (25+5) is very effective for deep learning
- Morning 6-9 AM is the golden time for memory, suitable for memorization
- Teaching others is the best way to learn (Feynman Technique)
- 2024-03: Found that combining practice is 3x more efficient than pure reading
- Reviewing 30 minutes before sleep improves memory by 40%
- Multi-dimensional learning (read + listen + write + speak) is 5x better than single method

## Methods

### Feynman Technique

Explain complex concepts in the simplest way to find understanding gaps.

1. Choose a concept
2. Imagine teaching it to a complete beginner
3. Explain in simple language, identify where you get stuck
4. Go back and study the stuck parts
5. Simplify language, use analogies and diagrams

### SQ3R Reading Method

Applicable for books and literature reading.

1. **Survey**: Quickly browse table of contents, headings, summaries
2. **Question**: Ask what you want to learn from it
3. **Read**: Read carefully with questions in mind
4. **Recite**: Recite key points in your own words
5. **Review**: Review and organize notes

### Deliberate Practice

Targeted practice for weak areas.

1. Identify specific weak areas
2. Design targeted practice tasks
3. Get immediate feedback
4. Repeat until muscle memory forms
